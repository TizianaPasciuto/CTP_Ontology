Abstract

This document describes an ontological module for the creation and structuring of cultural thematic paths, aimed at supporting the linked data publishing and managing for the fruition of cultural heritage.
The namespace for CTP terms is https://w3id.org/ctp#
The suggested prefix for CTP namespace is ctp.

In this module the following Ontology Design Patterns have been used:
  • http://www.ontologydesignpatterns.org/cp/owl/list.owl
  • http://www.ontologydesignpatterns.org/cp/owl/partof.owl
  • http://www.ontologydesignpatterns.org/cp/owl/place.owl

Cultural Thematic Path (CTP) Ontology: Description back to ToC

The creation of digital thematic paths represents an innovative approach to the enjoyment of online content, enabling users to navigate in a structured manner through information relevant to a specific topic and/or sector. In order to maximize the effectiveness of such paths, the intention is to develop an ontology that guides in their structure and organization.
The development of an ontology for thematic paths aims not only to provide a clear and comprehensive definition of the concept but also to specify its main and basic requirements and the organization of content. The main purpose is to facilitate the enhancement of cultural heritage by facilitating user navigation through a series of described and interconnected information and resources. This achievement can be attained through the development of an ontology that serves as a tool for the organization, management, exchange, and enjoyment of online information.
With regard to the scope, although the structuring of thematic paths can be related to any sector, in this study the attention will be focused on the GLAM domain. Therefore, the reference scope will be related to the cultural heritage.

ThematicPathc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/ctp#ThematicPath

A thematic path is a tool for the enjoyment of cultural heritage that allows to connect cultural objects of different nature (tangible and intangible) through the identification of common themes. The chosen theme, which may concern specific topics, contexts, events, places, historical periods or characters and so on, is expressed synthetically by a title and in-depth with a written descriptive text; they can be further identified through subjects. The thematic path could be structured either as a collector of cultural objects, without establishing an order of priority, or present a predetermined order. The development and use of this enjoyment strategy can be profitable to convey messages and cultural, ethical and social values.
is in domain of
allowsTheSaleOf op, conveys dp, hasCreationDate dp, hasPart op, hasSlot op, hasTheme op, includes op, isIncludedIn op, isInspiredByAgent op, isRealizedIn op, refersToAgent op, refersToPlace op, refersToTemporalEntity op
is in range of
agentRefersTo op, hasOrderedList op, includes op, inspires op, isIncludedIn op, isPartOf op, isThemeOf op, placeRefersTo op, realizes op, soldThrough op, temporalEntityRefersTo op
